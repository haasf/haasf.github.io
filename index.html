About Me

I am a rising Master's student in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Michigan through the Sequential Undergraduate Graduate Studies (SUGS) program. I'm currently working on applications of machine learning in healthcare as a researcher in the Machine Learning for Data-Driven Decisions (MLD3) lab under Prof. Jenna Wiens.

I recently finished my undergraduate studies in Data Science at U-M, graduating Summa Cum Laude. During my undergrad, I gained course experience in machine learning, computer vision/graphics, natural language processing, web systems, database management, generative models, science of large language models, and randomized numerical linear algebra for ML. Outside of my courses, I conducted research in adversarial ML for computer vision systems, was an instructional aide for EECS 445: Machine Learning, and was an officer for Tau Beta Pi, the Engineering Honor Society.

Besides academics, I have spent the last 4 summers working in restaurants where I've gone from a host/busser with no experience to leading a team of bartenders in a fine-dining waterfront restaurant. While non-technical, my experience in the service industry has played a significant part in my growth as a leader and has taught me how to manage high-pressure situations. While bartending and conducting research, I have also spent my last 2 summers playing drums for musicals at Interlochen Center for the Arts, a world-renowned music camp.
